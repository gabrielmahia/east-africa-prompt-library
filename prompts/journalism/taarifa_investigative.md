## Investigative Journalism — Data Analysis

**General prompt:**
"Analyze this dataset for newsworthy patterns."

**Formula:**
"Analyze [dataset type] from [source/institution] in Kenya for the period [dates]. Identify [anomalies/trends/disparities] that would be newsworthy for [audience]. Generate [story angles/follow-up questions/data visualization suggestions]."

**TaarifaAI examples:**
- "Analyze EACC corruption case data for 2020-2025 in Kenya. Identify which county governments appear most frequently, which sectors are most affected, and what the conviction rate is. Generate 3 story angles."
- "Review the Kenya National Assembly Hansard records for mentions of [controversial project] over the past 2 years. Identify which MPs spoke most frequently, their positions, and any contradictions with their voting records."
- "Analyze KNBS poverty data by county over 5 years. Identify counties where poverty is worsening despite increased CDF allocation. Generate an investigative story angle."

## Source Verification and Fact-Checking

**General prompt:**
"Verify the claims in this [document/statement/press release]."

**Formula:**
"Fact-check the following claim from [source] against [official statistics/court records/audit reports]: '[claim]'. Identify: (1) what is accurate, (2) what is misleading, (3) what is false, (4) what is missing context."

**East Africa examples:**
- "The Treasury CS claims Kenya's debt-to-GDP ratio is 'sustainable at 68%.' Fact-check this against IMF Article IV data and compare with peer African economies."
- "Verify this county government press release claiming '95% of bursary funds reached beneficiaries.' Cross-reference against the Controller of Budget report."
- "A politician claims the SGR 'paid for itself within 3 years.' Fact-check against Kenya Railways financial statements and the original Exim Bank loan terms."

## Swahili/Multilingual Journalism

**General prompt:**
"Write a news article about [event] in Swahili for a Kenyan audience."

**Formula:**
"Andika makala ya habari kuhusu [tukio] kwa hadhira ya [Kenya/East Africa]. Tumia lugha ya [Kiswahili sanifu/Sheng/lugha rahisi]. Jumuisha [quotes/takwimu/muktadha wa historia]."

**East Africa examples:**
- "Andika makala ya habari kuhusu ukame wa Turkana kwa hadhira ya vijijini ambayo haina intaneti. Tumia lugha rahisi ya Kiswahili. Jumuisha hatua za vitendo watu wanaweza kuchukua."
- "Write a bilingual (Swahili/English) explainer about how M-Pesa's new diaspora transfer product works, targeting Kenyan diaspora in the UK."
- "Summarize the Budget Statement 2026/27 in 500 words of plain English accessible to a Standard 8 reader in Kenya."

## Public Interest Reporting

**General prompt:**
"Generate story angles for an investigative piece about [institution]."

**Formula:**
"Generate [number] investigative story angles for a [news outlet/NGO/advocacy group] focusing on [institution/policy/sector] in Kenya. Each angle should: identify the public interest harm, name the documents needed, suggest FOIA/access to information requests, and list 5 sources to interview."

**East Africa examples:**
- "Generate 5 investigative story angles about the NHIF/SHA transition in Kenya. For each angle: identify the public harm, list documents to request, suggest officials to interview."
- "What are the 3 most underreported stories about land grabbing in Nairobi's informal settlements? For each: identify the legal mechanisms used, government bodies responsible, and communities affected."
- "Generate an investigative framework for examining contractor performance on Kenya's major road projects since 2015. Include: key metrics, data sources, FOIA requests, and visual storytelling opportunities."
